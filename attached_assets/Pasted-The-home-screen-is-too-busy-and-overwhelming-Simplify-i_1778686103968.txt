The home screen is too busy and overwhelming.
Simplify it completely — less is more.
This app is for elderly patients who need 
calm, clear, and simple screens.

---

### New home screen layout — clean version

Remove all of the following completely:
- The SOS floating button from the header
  (move it to Profile screen instead)
- The live sensor circle with RGB values
  — this is technical and confusing for patients
- The "LIVE SENSOR" label and R G B numbers
- The "Which wound should we monitor?" prompt
  — this should only appear once during setup

---

### What the home screen should show

Keep only these elements in this order:

1. HEADER (gradient purple):
   - Enzora logo centered
   - "Hello, [name]" bottom left
   - Arabic toggle top right
   - Clean, nothing else

2. CONNECTION STATUS PILL:
   - Green pill: "● Device Connected"
   - Red pill: "● Device Not Connected"
   - Small, subtle, just below header
   - No large red boxes or warnings

3. HERO STATUS CARD (the main focus):
   When connected and status is yellow:
   - Soft yellow/cream background
   - Large simple checkmark icon ✓
   - Big clear text: "Your wound looks normal"
   - Small subtitle: "Last checked: [time]"
   - Calm and reassuring, no extra information

   When status is green:
   - Soft warm background
   - Warning icon
   - "Early signs detected"
   - "Please monitor closely"

   When status is blue:
   - Soft red background  
   - Alert icon
   - "Please contact your doctor"
   - Gentle pulse animation only

   When not connected:
   - Simple message: "Connect your device 
     to start monitoring"
   - One button: "How to Connect?"

4. LAST READING:
   - Simple small text below the card:
     "Last reading: Today at 10:45 AM"
   - Nothing else

5. QUICK ACTION BUTTON:
   - One single button at the bottom:
     "View My Wounds →"

---

### Design rules for this screen

- Maximum 3 colors on the entire screen
- No icons that are not immediately obvious
- No technical terms (RGB, sensor, frequency)
- Font size minimum 16px everywhere
- Lots of white space — breathing room
- The status card should be the ONLY 
  thing the patient focuses on
- Everything else is secondary and small
